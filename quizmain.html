Description

Acute stress disorder, also known as "emotional shock", is a term to describe a person's first emotional response to having experienced or witnessed an extremely stressful situation. This might include an accident, death of a loved one, a sexual or physical assault, among others. Acute stress reactions are those experienced within 4 weeks of the traumatic event and may include initial emotional numbness, altered sense of oneself or surroundings, or impaired memories of the time around the event. These reactions may be followed by having mixed feelings about the event, upsetting thoughts or memories, and avoiding situations which may trigger distress. Normally, these symptoms get better within a few days. To support the normal healing process, affected people often benefit from contacting their loved ones, talking about the incident, doing activities which reduce stress and, in severe cases, taking medication.
